Anna's Lentils

FRESH
4 garlic cloves
2 yellow onions
2 carrots

PANTRY
2 cups brown or green lentils
6 bay leaves
1 bouillon cube
1 tsp red wine vinegar
3 tbsp olive oil
salt and pepper to taste

NICE-TO-HAVE:
½ tsp oregano

WATER
6 CUPS

In high school Natalie asked my mom to share her mango curry recipe. My mom wrote it down on a piece of paper, which I handed to Natalie the next day at school. The following day Natalie brought in the curry and it tasted nothing like my mom’s. Good enough to eat, though.

I think this is the first time this recipe has been written out, so it’s probably wrong. It follows a pretty typical Greek lentil recipe. My mom, Anna, can make this with her eyes closed, but couldn’t tell you the water-to-lentil-ratio. Her muscle memory precedes her. Good news is there are so few ingredients, it’s hard to mess up. Just add a shit ton of bay leaves and you’re good.

Heat olive oil in medium saucepan on low. Add diced garlic and onion and cook on medium-low heat until softened, Add water, bouillon cube, bay leaves, and diced carrot. (If you want to make this more of a soup, add more water.) Stir on medium heat to dissolve the bouillon. Add lentils and bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Add oregano and salt and pepper to taste. Cook for about 30 minutes until lentils are tender. Take off heat and add red wine vinegar before serving.
