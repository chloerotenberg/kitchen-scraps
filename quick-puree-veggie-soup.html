Quick Puree Veggie Soup

FRESH
1 head of broccoli
6 carrots
2 large zucchini
2 leeks
1 onion
1 medium/large Yukon Gold potato (optional)

PANTRY
Vegetable broth
Oil (olive or neutral oil like grapeseed or vegetable)
Salt + Pepper

NICE-TO-HAVE:
5-7 leaves of basil

WATER
? CUPS

The beauty of this recipe is that nothing needs to be chopped or measured very carefully. If you have more of some of these ingredients than others then that’s ok! It can also be a happy meeting place between baby food and soup and is surprisingly filling, especially with a slice of sourdough with salted butter! Hopefully, you haven’t paid $8 for a loaf at your local artisanal baker. My grandma makes this and my mom makes this and now I make this!

Heat olive or vegetable oil in large pot. Add diced onions and leeks. Cook until they are melty and just barely translucent. Add chopped carrots and broccoli stems (waste not wont not -- you’re broke bitch!). For a creamier ultimate soup texture, add the chopped potato. Cook until the colors are bright and the vegetables soften. Add broccoli crowns, chopped zucchini and sautee for another 8-10 minutes, mix occasionally. Add vegetable broth and water to cover vegetables and let simmer until soft. Salt and pepper to taste.

Add basil and take your immersion blender and puree that baby into soup! Add more water if needed, season with salt.

This recipe makes a lot of soup. You can eat it all rent week and/or freeze some for your next period of penny-pinching!
